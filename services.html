Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Not sure which survey you need? Here are answers to common questions from our clients.

What is the difference between a cadastral survey and a topographic survey?
A cadastral survey deals with legal property boundaries — it establishes, confirms, or records who owns what land, and produces documents accepted by the Deeds Registry and Surveyor General. A topographic survey records the physical features of the land — contours, buildings, trees, services — and is used as a base for design and planning. Both types are common in our practice, and we'll advise which is right for your specific project.
Do I need a survey before I can subdivide my property?
Yes. Any formal subdivision of land in South Africa requires a survey by a registered Professional Land Surveyor. The surveyor prepares a subdivision diagram (or general plan) which is lodged with the relevant Surveyor General's office, and a notarial deed is subsequently registered at the Deeds Registry. Without this step, the subdivision cannot be formally recognised. Contact us early in your subdivision process — we can advise on timing and requirements.
How long does a survey take?
Turnaround time depends on the survey type and project complexity. A simple boundary check or topographic survey of a residential property might be completed in 2–5 working days. Subdivision surveys requiring Surveyor General lodgement can take several weeks to months due to the government approval process — though the field and office work is typically done quickly on our end. We'll give you a realistic timeline with every proposal.
What is a "depiction" in land surveying?
A depiction is a technical drawing produced by a registered surveyor that shows the relationship between a building (or other structure) and the property boundaries. Depictions are often required by municipalities when applying for building plan approval — they verify that a building complies with the building lines and setback requirements registered against the title deed. We produce depictions for both new builds and existing structures.
Can you help with a dispute about a boundary between my property and my neighbour's?
Yes, this is one of our most common requests. We locate and re-establish the original survey beacons (pegs) using the registered survey diagram from the Surveyor General's records, and produce a certified professional opinion on the position of the boundary. This documentation is often used to resolve disputes without litigation — or as evidence if legal proceedings are necessary. Contact us for a consultation.
